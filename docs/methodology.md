# Methodology

Race Signals turns official FEC campaign finance records into a reporter-friendly feed.

The app is designed to surface leads. It does not make legal conclusions.

## Source

The MVP uses the Federal Election Commission OpenFEC API.

Initial scope:

- Federal candidates and committees
- 2026 cycle
- U.S. House
- Indiana

## What The App Collects

The first ingestion path should collect:

- Candidates
- Candidate-committee relationships
- Committee profiles
- Candidate totals
- Committee totals
- Filings
- Itemized receipts
- Itemized disbursements
- Independent expenditures

## What The App Does Not Collect Yet

The MVP does not include:

- State campaign finance data
- Meta ad data
- Google ad data
- FCC political files
- OpenSecrets data
- Polling
- Election results
- Voter files

## Data Freshness

Race Signals should display the latest successful ingestion time.

FEC processed data can lag. Electronic filings may take days to be categorized and coded. Paper filings can take weeks.

For that reason, the app should avoid calling itself real-time. "Latest available from FEC" is more accurate.

## Signal Generation

Signals are generated by deterministic rules.

Each signal stores:

- Rule ID
- Event date
- Source record
- Source link
- Plain-English title
- Plain-English explanation

The first rules should include:

- New candidate
- New committee
- New filing
- Amended filing
- Large receipt
- Large disbursement
- Independent expenditure supporting a candidate
- Independent expenditure opposing a candidate
- Repeated activity by the same committee in a short window

## Contributor Data

Itemized contributor records do not represent every donor.

Some donors may not appear individually because of reporting thresholds or aggregation rules.

Race Signals should not display contributor street addresses.

The UI may show:

- Contributor name
- City
- State
- Employer
- Occupation
- Amount
- Date
- Committee
- Source link

## Candidate And Committee Caveats

Candidate and committee relationships can change.

Committees may amend reports.

Candidate names, committee names, party labels and office-seeking status can be messy in source data.

The app should preserve original FEC IDs and source links so a reporter can verify the record.

## Editorial Limits

A signal means "look here."

It does not mean:

- Wrongdoing occurred
- A race is competitive
- A donor controls a candidate
- A candidate coordinated with outside spending
- A filing is complete or final

The feed should be written for curiosity and verification, not accusation.
